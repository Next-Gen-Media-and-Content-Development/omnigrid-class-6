What is Science?
🍃 Naru says — Believe it! Did you know the word "Science" is over 600 years old? It comes from the Latin word "Scientia" — meaning pure knowledge. Every question you ask is the very beginning of science! 🔬

Science is a systematic and organised body of knowledge gained through observation and experiments. It helps us understand the natural world around us.

The word "Science" comes from the Latin word "Scientia" — which means "Knowledge".

Science has three main branches:

  • 🔵 Physics — Study of force, energy, motion and matter
  • 🟢 Chemistry — Study of the composition and reactions of matter
  • 🟡 Biology — Study of living organisms

💡 Key Insight

The most important quality in science is curiosity! Every great discovery began with a simple question — "Why?" or "How?"
🍃 NARU'S FUN FACT
The word "Hypothesis" comes from Greek — hypo (under) + thesis (placing). It literally means "putting something forward to be tested"! Scientists have been doing this for over 2,000 years. 🌟

Scientific Method
⚡ Sasu says — with precision Every scientist in the world follows these exact same steps — from NASA engineers to your school lab. The Scientific Method is the universal code of science. Master it. 🎯

The Scientific Method is a systematic step-by-step process used by scientists to solve problems and make new discoveries.

🔄 Observation → Question → Hypothesis → Experiment → Data → Conclusion

Steps of the Scientific Method:

  • 👀 Observation: Notice something interesting using your senses
  • ❓ Question: Ask "Why?" or "How?" about what you observed
  • 💡 Hypothesis: Form a testable prediction or educated guess
  • ⚗️ Experiment: Design and conduct a controlled test
  • 📊 Data Collection: Record and organise your results carefully
  • ✅ Conclusion: Analyse data — was your hypothesis correct?

⚠️ Important!

Even if a hypothesis is proved wrong — that is still a valuable result! In science, there is no failure, only learning 🌱
⚡ SASU'S FACT
Galileo Galilei (1564–1642) was one of the first scientists to use the Scientific Method systematically. He proved that objects of different weights fall at the same speed — by dropping balls from the Leaning Tower of Pisa! 🗼

Laboratory Safety Rules
🛡️ Naru says — Don't be reckless! A true ninja never charges in without preparation! Same in the lab — always wear your safety glasses and apron before anything else. Recklessness is a ninja's worst enemy! ⚗️

Safety is the most important priority when working in a laboratory. Chemicals and equipment can be dangerous if not handled carefully.

🛡️ Laboratory Safety Rules:

  • 👓 Always wear safety glasses and a lab apron
  • 🚫 Never taste or smell any chemical directly
  • 👩‍🏫 Perform experiments only under teacher supervision
  • 🧼 Wash your hands thoroughly after every experiment
  • 🍎 No eating or drinking is allowed inside the laboratory
  • 🚨 Report any accident immediately to your teacher
  • 🔥 Never wear loose clothing near an open flame

Famous Scientists
🏆 Naru & Sasu say Naru: India gave the world Zero and calculated Pi — that's like unlocking an ultimate jutsu! 🍃
Sasu: And C.V. Raman won the Nobel Prize in Physics. Indian science has a legacy worthy of respect. ⚡

India has made extraordinary contributions to science. Here are some key scientists you must know at Class 6 level:

  • 🏅 C.V. Raman — Discovered the Raman Effect (1928) • Awarded Nobel Prize in Physics 1930
  • 🚀 APJ Abdul Kalam — Led India's missile technology and space programme
  • ⚛️ Homi Bhabha — Founded India's nuclear research programme
  • 🔢 Aryabhata — Introduced the concept of Zero (0) and calculated the value of Pi
  • 🌟 Srinivasa Ramanujan — One of the greatest mathematical geniuses in history

🌟 Did You Know?

C.V. Raman was the first Asian to win the Nobel Prize in Physics — in the year 1930!

Eureka Moment!
Over 2,200 years ago, Archimedes reportedly discovered the principle of buoyancy while sitting in his bath — and ran through the streets shouting "Eureka!" (I have found it!) 🏃
🍎
Newton and the Apple!
According to legend, seeing an apple fall from a tree inspired Newton to develop his theory of gravity — but the full theory took years of rigorous work to complete! 😄
🏆
C.V. Raman — Nobel Laureate!
In 1930, C.V. Raman became the first Asian to win the Nobel Prize in Physics. He made his groundbreaking discovery using relatively simple equipment — proof that great science needs curiosity more than fancy labs!
🔢
The Magic of Zero!
Aryabhata introduced the concept of Zero in the 5th century CE. Without zero, modern mathematics, computers and the internet as we know them simply could not exist! 🤯
🧪
Marie Curie — Double Nobel!
Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize — and she won it twice! Once in Physics (1903) and once in Chemistry (1911). She remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences. 🏆🏆
🌍
Science is Everywhere!
From the moment you wake up to the moment you sleep — you are surrounded by science! The toothpaste you use, the fan that keeps you cool, the food you eat — all of it is science in action! 🌟
